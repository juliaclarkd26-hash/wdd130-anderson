Filing Resources: Where to Get Help

You don’t have to be a tax expert to file a successful return. Depending on how much you earned and how complex your finances are, there are several tools available to help you. We’ve broken these down into Free Resources (best for most students) and Paid Resources (best for those with unique financial situations).

Free Resources

Most BYU-I students will qualify for at least one of these free options.

BYU-I Volunteer Tax Review Center

  • What it is: A service provided by the BYU-Idaho Department of Accounting where student volunteers (accounting majors) review your prepared return for accuracy.
  • The Perk: They often provide a special code to file your Idaho State return for free if you use their partner software.
  • Who should use it: Any student who wants a second pair of eyes on their return before they hit "submit."
  • Where: Located in the Smith Building (SMI), typically open from late January through March.

IRS Free File

  • What it is: A partnership between the IRS and brand-name tax software providers. If your income is below a certain limit (usually $79,000–$89,000), you can use their full-featured software for $0.
  • Who should use it: Students with a standard income who want a guided, step-by-step digital experience without the price tag of commercial software.

FreeTaxUSA

  • What it is: A highly-rated tax website that offers free federal filing for almost everyone, regardless of complexity.
  • Who should use it: Students who have slightly more complex situations (like a small side business or stock sales) but still don't want to pay for the "premium" tiers of bigger brands.

Paid Resources

While these charge a fee, they often offer more robust support and specialized tools.

TurboTax or H&R Block (Paid Tiers)

  • What it is: The most popular tax software platforms. They are very user-friendly and can import your W-2 data automatically from many large employers.
  • The Cost: Usually ranges from $60 to $120+ depending on the complexity of your forms.
  • Who should use it: Students who value extreme convenience, have multiple high-value investments, or own a complex business and want "Audit Defense" or live expert chat support.

Local Rexburg CPA (Certified Public Accountant)

  • What it is: A professional tax preparer who does the work for you from start to finish.
  • The Cost: Typically the most expensive option, often starting at $150–$300+.
  • Who should use it: Students who are married with complicated business holdings, own rental properties, or have complex multi-state filing requirements that software might struggle with.

Note on State Taxes

Idaho requires its own tax return. While federal filing is often free, many commercial sites charge a separate fee (around $40–$60) for the state return. Always check if the BYU-I Tax Review Center has a discount code before you pay!
