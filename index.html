BYU-I Tax Help: Taxes Simplified for Students

Filing taxes can feel like navigating a Rexburg roundabout for the first time—confusing and slightly stressful. Whether you are a freshman with your first part-time job or a senior prepping for graduation, this site is designed to help you understand the basics of filing without the headache.

Should I File My Taxes?

The most common question students ask is: "Do I even have to do this?" Generally, you should file if any of the following apply to you:

  • You earned more than $14,600: For the 2025 tax year, if your gross income exceeded this amount, the IRS requires you to file a return.
  • You want your money back: Even if you made less than the threshold, you should file if your employer withheld federal income tax from your paychecks. Filing is the only way to get that refund.
  • You qualify for credits: Many students qualify for the American Opportunity Tax Credit (AOTC), which can reduce your tax bill or even put money back in your pocket just for being a student.

Important Dates to Remember

Don't let the deadline sneak up on you between midterms and finals.

  • Tax Season Opens: Late January 2026
  • The Big Deadline: Tuesday, April 15, 2026
  • Note: This is the final day to submit your federal and Idaho state returns without a penalty.
